## UX Writing Principles to Apply

### Clarity First
- Use simple, direct language
- Avoid jargon unless industry-appropriate
- One main idea per sentence
- Active voice when possible

### Compelling & Engaging
- Strong action verbs
- Benefit-focused (what's in it for the reader?)
- Specific rather than vague
- Show, don't just tell

### SEO-Conscious
- Integrate relevant keywords naturally
- Use searchable terminology
- Strategic word choice for discoverability

### Brand-Aligned
- Match the voice defined in style guide
- Consistent tone across all content
- Authentic and genuine
- Appropriate for target audience

### Accessibility
- Clear and scannable
- Descriptive headings
- Meaningful link text
- Logical information hierarchy

## Content-Specific Best Practices

### Section Headers
- Clear and descriptive (not clever)
- Action-oriented or benefit-focused
- Scannable at a glance
- Consistent structure across sections

### Process Descriptions
- Balance detail with conciseness
- Use clear steps or phases
- Explain "why" not just "what"
- Show your thinking and approach

### Outcome Statements
- Quantify when possible (metrics, %)
- Focus on impact and value
- Connect design decisions to results
- Use specific examples

### Role Descriptions
- Lead with impact and ownership
- Use active voice ("Led," "Designed," "Drove")
- Specific rather than generic
- Show scope and collaboration

### Calls-to-Action
- Clear next step
- Benefit-oriented
- Friendly but professional
- Remove friction
